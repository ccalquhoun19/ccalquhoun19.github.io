The Big Day!

*Written from my wife's perspective*

The big day had finally come! All the planning and preparation had been leading up to this one day and it couldn't have been more perfect. There were no last-minute scrambling or mistakes that needed to be fixed. The only minor hiccups caused no stress and were taken care of in less than 5 minutes. Camden and I were both as nervous and excited as possible (Camden said he only got 3 hours of sleep the night before). I can remember waking up in the morning and feeling nervous, excited, yet still at peace and calm. I headed to the church where Camden and I would get ready in separate buildings, until 10:00am when we would have our private first look before the ceremony. It didn't feel any different than when we saw each other normally. Of course, we were in our wedding best, but I knew this was the same Camden I've known and wanted to marry all along. The ceremony started shortly after our first look. Our friends and family supported us as we made our vows to each other and to God. After that we danced (literally) down the aisle in celebration! After that there was cake, toasts, dancing, celebration, and laughter. To be honest, I don't remember much detail, but I remember the feeling and excitement of being with friends and family. Finally, we drove off in Camden's 1997 Honda Accord as our guests threw flower petals and cheered us on as we started our new adventure.

Getting Settled

*Back to Camden*

After the honeymoon we moved into our first house in Olympia. The first month was a little bumpy as the honeymoon feeling started to wear off and adulting hit us. We were both working full time, with her schedule changing week and me working overtime weekly. Despite this, we grew together as we figured out life together. That was the #1 thing I was looking forward to in marriage, and that's exactly what we did. We were officially living on our own and off our own income. We were also blessed to have family and friends all living nearby to support us with anything we needed help with. After the summer we prepared to move down to Yamhill for me to start school and start the next chapter.

Current Lessons

I've learned so much these past four months. The most important thing I've learned is taking note of what my wife finds important and enjoys doing. Despite the fact it might not be something I enjoy as much, I know she finds value in wrapping aspects of her life up with me. I've also had to exercise goal setting and planning in general. It takes a lot for us to get through a day with energy and making sure we've checked off everything we need to. Overall, I feel like it's going pretty well, especially getting to start and end each day with my best friend. What a blessing our journey has been.
