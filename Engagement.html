Thankful to be Together

After the first year of doing long-distance, we both loved the idea of being a 1 minute walk away from each other. I was in Beebe suites and she was in Edwards. Despite the difficulties Covid provided for a dating couple such as uncertainties of whether we were even allowed to hold hands since we didn't live together, we found ways to spend time together. We had study sessions, numerous walks around the Quad, more than a handful of Super Smash Bros. tournaments, and even changing the alternator in her car. We couldn't have been more thankful to be together during that time.

Bumps Along the Road

Everyone was so excited we were finally engaged, but then the unexpected happened. Only a few weeks after getting engaged I had some baggage from the past come up. It totally shook me and took me by surprise, not to mention adding in everything else that was going on at the time. I was struggling to juggle everything at the time and my fiancee, hoping to take an issue off my plate (I had briefly mentioned getting married was a little stressful), gave me back the ring. Long story short, I was able to figure my stuff out with her still by my side and we got formally engaged again. Despite the difficulty at the time, that particular season only grew us closer together and closer to God.

Large Holes in the Road

All seemed to be going well as we finished up Fall semester. We headed home to Olympia for a much-needed Winter break. During that time, we decided, much to our initial dislike, that school wasn't the best course of action for her. She stayed home in Olympia while I reluctantly returned to campus alone. We were used to long distance but being apart after so much time together made it more difficult than anticipated. We did our best though. Then the big bump came. Out of nowhere, she informed me there were two big deficiencies she saw in me that made her unsure if our original wedding date of June 12 would be too soon. I felt like my world was crumbling around me. I didn't see it coming. Arguably the most difficult time of my life, I sought God and advice from others like never before. It was all an uphill battle in the midst of finals approaching, being long-distance, unexpectedly getting Covid, and more on top of that. I had no idea how I would make it through.

The Last Steps!

God turned the situation into a masterpiece and a true testimony. Long-distance helped us process what was happening individually and with others. Classes gave me a good distraction from the situation. Covid even led to me living at her house with her mom for 2 weeks, giving us numerous opportunities to finally have those hard talks. Long story short, we decided to keep the wedding for June 12. After school ended, we were both working full time and doing all the fun wedding planning. Fast forward a month, and the day we had fought tooth and nail for was finally upon us. Hallelujah!
